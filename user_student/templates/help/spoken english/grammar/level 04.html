Grammar - Level 4 Lesson

Adjectives

Adjectives are words that describe the nouns. Adjectives are words that modify or describe nouns. enormous, doglike, silly, yellow, fun, fast.
They can also describe the quantity of nouns: many, few, millions, eleven.

In some sentences, adjectives are easy to identify because they come immediately before the nouns they modify.

They can also act as a complement to linking verbs or the verb to be. A linking verb is a verb like to feel, to seem, or to taste that describes a state of being

Adjectives that modify the noun

  • Mahesh wore a beautiful hat to the dance contest.
  • My cake should have sixteen candles.
  • The scariest villain of all time is Puri.

Adjectives that act as a complement to linking verb

  • That cow is happy.
  • It smells gross in the storeroom.
  • Driving is faster than walking.
