Sentences – Level 3 Lesson

Compound Sentence

A compound sentence is formed when you join two main clauses with a connective. In a compound sentence the clauses are linked by a conjunctions (and, but, so, or).

  • I like bananas and I like grapes.
  • Chandini can be naughty at times but she is a nice girl.

Compound Sentence = main clause +connective +main clause

We could separate the compound sentence into two smaller, complete thoughts

Compound Sentence = main clause +connective +main clause

  • Senthil enjoys watching movies. Sridevi enjoys watching movies too. -> Senthil and Sridevi both enjoy watching movies
  • I like to walk in the park. I also like watching the ducks there. -> I like to walk in the park and watch the ducks there.

Sentences – Level 3 Examples
