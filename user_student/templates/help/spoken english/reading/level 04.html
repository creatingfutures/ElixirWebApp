Reading – Level 4

This level is for students who can use their phonics knowledge and read more complex words. In this level you may choose to read silently or read out loud.

Examples

Some words ending with -ing, -er which might be tricky to read.

  • Quick-er
  • Fast-er
  • Eat-ing
  • I'll instead of I will
  • I'm instead of I am<< /li>

Few tricky words, for example knife (k is silent).

Stolen Wallet

It is interval time in your school. Everyone is out on the playing fields, including the teacher. You had to go to the toilet. On the way to the toilet, you pass your class and notice that there is someone in the room. You stop and peek in out of curiosity. Just as you do, you see one of your best friends reach into another person’s desk and take something out of it. You quickly move past the door before you are seen.

Just before the lunch bell rings, the students whose desk you saw your friend reach into, walks up to the teacher. A moment later the teacher announces that this student’s entire wallet with money, cards and other aspects in it has been stolen. What do you do?
