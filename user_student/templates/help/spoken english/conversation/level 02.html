Conversation – Level 2

To converse effectively, one would need to focus on the following:

  • Listening
  • Understanding
  • Articulating
  • Clarifying
  • Asking Questions

Example

Below is an example of a set of conversations of a tourist. This includes asking Questions, Listening, Responding

Being A Tourist

  • You can’t find the town center in Bangalore. What do you ask someone?
    • Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to the town center?
  • How do you ask for the Tourist Information center?
    • Excuse me, could you tell me where the Tourist information Centre is?
  • Someone asks you for directions. What do you say?
    • I’m afraid i can’t help you. I’m a stranger here myself.
  • You need to use a toilet. What do you ask someone?
    • Excuse me; are there any public toilets near here?
  • Someone asks you how long you’re staying. How do you say you’re on a day trip?
    • I’m just here for the day.
  • You decide to stay overnight but don’t want to spend too much. What do you ask at the Tourist Information?
    • Could you please recommend a reasonable priced hotel?
  • Ask the distance to the hotel.
    • How far is it from here?
  • Ask how to get there
    • Could you give me directions, please?
  • He tells you the address but you’d like it on a piece of paper. What do you ask?
    • Could you write it down for me, please?
  • How do you ask a travel agent about holidays in Paris?
    • Could you give me some information about holidays in Paris, please?
