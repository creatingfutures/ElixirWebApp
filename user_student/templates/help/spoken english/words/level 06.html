Words-Level 6 Lesson

Word Formation

There are four main kinds of word formation:prefixes, suffixes, conversion and compounds.

     Prefixes

We add prefixes before the base of a word.
Examples Prefixes
monorail, monolingual mono- means 'one'
multipurpose, multicultural multi- means 'many'
post-war, postgraduate post- means 'after'
unusual, undemocratic un- means 'not' or 'opposite to'

     Suffixes

We add suffixes after the base or stem of a word.The main purpose of a suffix is to show what class of word it is(eg.noun or adjective).
Examples Suffixes
terrorism, freedom -ism and -dom are used to form nouns
employer, actor -er and -or are used to form nouns to describe people who do things.
widen, simplify -en and -fy are used to form verbs
reasonable, unprofitable -ly is a common suffix used to form adverbs

      Conversion

Conversion involves the change of a word from one word class to another. For example, the verbs to email and to microwave are formed from the nouns email and microwave:

  • Can you text her? (verb from noun text, meaning to send a text-message)
  • They are always jetting somewhere. (verb from noun jet)
  • If you’re not careful, some downloads can damage your computer. (noun from verb download)
  • OK, so the meeting’s on Tuesday. That’s a definite. (noun from adjective)
  • It’s a very big if and I’m not at all sure we can afford it. (noun from conjunction, meaning ‘it’s not at all certain’)
  • All companies have their ups and downs. (nouns from prepositions)

      Compounding

When we use compounding, we link together two or more words to create a new word. Normally, the first item identifies a key feature of the second word.
For example, the two words back and ache can combine to form the compound noun "backache", and the two words post and card combine to form the compound noun "postcard".
Example: car park, rock band
