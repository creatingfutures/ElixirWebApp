Types of Verbs

Verbs are action words, like shout, jump, run, and eat. They tell us what’s happening in the sentence.

There are three types of verbs:

Action verbs (which can be transitive or intransitive)

These are the ones that are used to talk about the different actions that are happening and the physical activities that a subject might perform.

Example -

  • taste – This apple tastes good.
  • hear – I can hear the children crying.

Modal verbs (sometimes called helping verbs)

Modal verbs help to ask for permission and when making requests. They are used to express ability, possibility, or obligation. Each one of the modal verbs can be used to express one or more of these modalities. They can also be used to form the future tense and to make conditional sentences.

Example -

  • Can you come home early today?
  • It could rain tomorrow

Auxiliary verbs (sometimes called linking verbs)

Auxiliary (or Helping) verbs are used together with a main verb to show the verb’s tense or to form a negative or question. The most common auxiliary verbs are have, be, and do.

Example -

  • She was baking a cake for dessert.
  • Dad has been working hard all day.

Words-Level 5 Examples

Action verbs

  • Ride, Sit down, Stand up, Fight, Laugh, Read, Play, Listen, Cry, Think

Model verbs

  • Can, May, Must, Shall, Will, Ought to, Need, be to, Have to, Would, Should, Used to
  • Could I go play with Sammer?
  • I can call you later
  • May I help you with anything?
  • Ira might need your help later
  • Look at the sky, it won't rain later.
  • Will you turn off the light?

Auxiliary verbs

  • Am, Is, are, was, were, Be, Been, Will, Has, Have, Had, Do, Does, Did
  • Does Sam write all his own reports?
  • The clerks haven't written all the letters yet.
  • Abdul is writing an e-mail to a client at the moment.
